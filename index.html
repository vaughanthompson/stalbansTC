About Us

St Albans Tennis Club is a friendly tennis club that welcomes all age groups and abilities.

The Club has six tennis Courts (with keypad code access): four Plexipave courts, two Asphalt courts. Clubrooms have great facilities and BBQ. Free onsite parking.



Hours of play

Club Day: Saturdays from 1pm until approximately 5pm (Clubroom drinks and banter if you feel like staying on).

Twilight Tennis: Thursdays - Summer from 5pm. Winter from 3pm.

Interclub Teams: SATC is affiliated with Tennis Canterbury 2021; we can offer Junior Interclub on Friday afternoons or Saturday mornings. Weekend Senior Interclub (currently combining with Avonhead Tennis Club). Note: Interclub team registrations close in early September each season.

Coaching


Provided by Nick Caton Tennis Ltd, at St Albans courts.
Head Coach: Nick Caton and Development Coach: Rhys Weaver

Nick moved to Christchurch in 2021 after previously holding Head Coach roles in the UK, Europe, Nelson, and Wellington. He competed internationally and nationally as a Junior and had the honour of being the first person from Whanganui to represent New Zealand.

Nick's passion for tennis led to an easy transition from playing, to coaching. He loves working with all ages and abilities and takes great satisfaction in helping each player become the best version of themselves they can be, on court.

Junior Hotshots

(Included in Junior Membership)

Focusing on introductory concepts and tennis fundamentals. Held during School Terms 1 and 4, for 16 weeks.

Sessions are run in small groups, based on age and ability:

  • Red ball (5-8 years). Build fundamental and perceptual motor skills, key grips, ground strokes, serve, volley and court awareness and game understanding 45 mins.
  • Orange (8-10 years). Progress stroke technique, directional hitting, serving rhythm, basic tactical awareness, and implementation.
  • Green (10-12 years). Advance tactical knowledge, spin on ground strokes and serve. 60 mins. Teen Beginner Squad. Build tactical understanding, fundamental technique on all strokes, single and doubles game understanding.
